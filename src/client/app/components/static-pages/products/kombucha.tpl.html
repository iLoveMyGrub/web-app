An ancient probiotic elixir

What is kombucha? 

This curious drink is made by fermenting sweet tea using a kombucha culture known as a ‘SCOBY’ (Symbiotic Culture of Bacteria and Yeasts). This little culture transforms the flavour of the tea into something quite unusual. Kombucha is a tart, refreshing drink with a complex depth of flavour and its own natural carbonation. Once fermenting is finished, the kombucha is left teeming with probiotics. Enjoyed for centuries in China and Russia it’s often referred to anecdotally as the ‘elixir of life’.

We second ferment our kombucha to increase its natural fizz and probiotic content. Our kombucha is raw (unpasturised), preserving the friendly bacteria that proliferate during the fermentation process.

Kombucha benefits

  • Aids digestion
  • Helps maintain a healthy gut
  • Contains billions of probiotics
  • Boosts energy/mood naturally

How much should you drink?

It’s good to start slowly with live, fermented drinks as they’re a rich source of probiotics. We suggest starting with 4oz of kombucha a day (about half a mug). If you drink a small amount at mealtimes, the enzymes and probiotics in the kombucha will help digest your food.

About Bednářová

Set up by food journalist Helenka Bednar, Bednářová came about through various experiments in her kitchen, creating all kinds of fermented foods. She delved into the intriguing world of fermentation in a bid to help her dodgy digestion, and got hooked on the tangy flavours and the positive effect these live foods had on her health. Passionate about food, nutrition and health, Helenka is particularly interested in the link between a healthy gut, and a healthy body and mind.

People often say ‘you are what you eat’, but at Bednářová we believe that ‘you are what you absorb’. Every person has around 2 pounds of bacteria in their gut, and most of them should be the friendly type! These friendly bacteria are the guardians of your digestive system. They help to break down your food, absorb nutrients, protect your gut lining and even produce vitamins for your body to utilise. Consuming fermented foods on a regular basis is a tasty way to top up your friendly bacteria.
